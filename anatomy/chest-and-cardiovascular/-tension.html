EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pressure is an important concept in physics, and is defined as the magnitude of force acting perpendicular to a surface, per unit area. The SI unit for pressure is the pascal (Pa), defined as 1 newton per meter squared.

Concept note-2: -(preʃəʳ ) uncountable noun. If you are experiencing pressure, you feel that you must do a lot of tasks or make a lot of decisions in very little time, or that people expect a lot from you. Collins COBUILD Advanced Learner’s Dictionary.

Concept note-3: -What Is the SI Unit of Pressure? The SI unit of pressure is pascal (represented as Pa) which is equal to one newton per square metre (N/m2 or kg m-1s-2). Interestingly, this name was given in 1971. Before that pressure in SI was measured in newtons per square metre.
