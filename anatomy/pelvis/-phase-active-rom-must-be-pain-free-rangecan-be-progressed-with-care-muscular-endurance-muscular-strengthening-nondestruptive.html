EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This concise review provides a sight about the various phases of muscle repair and regeneration, namely degeneration, inflammation, regeneration, remodeling and maturation.

Concept note-2: -Phase 3: Remodelling Phase In this phase, your body starts to teach the scar tissue to behave like the tissue it has replaced. It produces new additional tissue to help strengthen and support the healed scar tissue so you can keep up with the demands of your normal physical activity.

Concept note-3: -There are a number of types of muscle injury that can occur: laceration, contusion, degenerative diseases (eg Muscular Dystrophies) and strain.

Concept note-4: -Tendon healing after surgical repair generally progresses through a short inflammatory phase, which lasts about a week, followed by a proliferative phase, which lasts a few weeks, followed by a remodeling phase, which lasts many months.
