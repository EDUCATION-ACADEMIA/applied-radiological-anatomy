THE MUSCLES THAT PERFORM EXTERNAL ROTATION OF THE THIGH ARE ALL EXCEPT

(A) EXTERNAL SHUTTER

(B) PYRAMIDAL OF THE PELVIS

(C) INTERNAL SHUTTER

(D) ** GLUTEUS MINUS

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Obturator externus externally rotates and helps adduct the thigh at the hip joint. It also stabilizes the head of the femur.

Concept note-2: -With the hip extended, gluteus minimus externally rotates the thigh.

Concept note-3: -The gluteus minimus acts in synergy with the gluteus medius to abduct and internally rotate the thigh, and contributes to the stabilization of the hip and pelvis.

Concept note-4: -The lateral rotators are: the superior gemellus, inferior gemellus, obturator externus, obturator internus, quadratus femoris, and the piriformis.
