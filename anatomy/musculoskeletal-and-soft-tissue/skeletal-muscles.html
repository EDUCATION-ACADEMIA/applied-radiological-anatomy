Skeletal Muscles

(A) The gradual weakening of muscles.

(B) ** The voluntary muscles that attach to bones.

(C) The involuntary muscles that attach to bones.

(D) The gradual strengthening of muscles.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Skeletal muscles comprise 30 to 40% of your total body mass. Theyâ€™re the muscles that connect to your bones and allow you to perform a wide range of movements and functions. Skeletal muscles are voluntary, meaning you control how and when they work.

Concept note-2: -skeletal muscle, also called voluntary muscle, in vertebrates, most common of the three types of muscle in the body. Skeletal muscles are attached to bones by tendons, and they produce all the movements of body parts in relation to each other.

Concept note-3: -Skeletal muscles are under our conscious control, which is why they are also known as voluntary muscles.

Concept note-4: -Voluntary muscles are skeletal muscles that attach to bones and can be consciously activated to control movement. Common voluntary skeletal muscles include the biceps, triceps, lats, abdominals, glutes, quadriceps, and hamstrings.

Concept note-5: -Skeletal muscle is attached by cord-like tendons to bone, such as in the legs, arms, and face. Skeletal muscles are called striated (pronounced: STRY-ay-ted) because they are made up of fibers that have horizontal stripes when viewed under a microscope.
