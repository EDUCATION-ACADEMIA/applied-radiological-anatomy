This part of the brain is involved in initial stages of visual processing, including dreams and hallucinations.

(A) ** occipital lobe

(B) frontal lobe

(C) parietal lobe

(D) temporal lobe

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The posteriormost part of the occipital lobe is known as the occipital pole. The occipital lobe is primarily responsible for visual processing. It contains the primary and association visual cortex.

Concept note-2: -The striate cortex is involved in the initial cortical processing of all visual information necessary for visual perception and its damage results in loss of vision in the contralesional hemifield.

Concept note-3: -The occipital lobes sit at the back of the head and are responsible for visual perception, including colour, form and motion. Damage to the occipital lobe can include: Difficulty with locating objects in environment. Difficulty with identifying colours (Colour Agnosia)

Concept note-4: -Your occipital lobe, the smallest and rear-most of the lobes, is the visual processing hub of your brain. This area processes visual signals and works cooperatively with many other brain areas.

Concept note-5: -Occipital lobe. The occipital lobe is the back part of the brain that is involved with vision.
