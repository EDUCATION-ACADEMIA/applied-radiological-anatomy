What is a sign of respiratory distress?

(A) Client has decreased restlessness.

(B) Client has a decreased pulse rate.

(C) Client has respiration rate of 18 breaths per minute.

(D) ** Client has blue-coloured mucous membranes.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cyan means ‘blue’ and the abnormal bluish discoloration of the skin and mucous membranes is referred to as “cyanosis”. It is a pathologic sign, and not a disease by itself. Underlying diseases that increase the deoxygenated hemoglobin to 5.0 g/dl or more leads to cyanosis.

Concept note-2: -Cyanosis is a bluish, grayish, or purplish cast to the skin and mucous membranes. It is a sign that your blood isn’t carrying enough oxygen.

Concept note-3: -Cyanosis that affects the skin generally and/or lips It is usually a sign of low levels of oxygen in the blood. Common causes for central cyanosis are listed below. A problem with the lungs: worsening of a long-term lung condition, such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
