In what stage of urine formation is useful solutes returned to blood?

(A) ** tubular reabsorption

(B) tubular secretion

(C) glomerular filtration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tubular reabsorption is the second major step in urine formation. Most of the reabsorption of solutes necessary for normal body function such as amino acids, glucose, and salts takes place in the proximal part of the tubule.

Concept note-2: -Tubular reabsorption is the process that moves solutes and water out of the filtrate and back into your bloodstream.

Concept note-3: -In tubular reabsoption, the second step of urine formation, almost all nutrients are reabsorbed in the renal tubule by active or passive transport.

Concept note-4: -During tubular reabsorption, cells of the tubule reabsorb almost all water and a variety of solutes from the filtrate as it flows through the renal tubule and collecting duct. These reabsorbed substances are then returned to the blood in the peritubular capillaries and vasa recta.

Concept note-5: -The next step in urine formation is tubular secretion. Here, tubular cells secrete substances like hydrogen ions, potassium ions, etc into the filtrate. Through this process, the ionic, acid-base and the balance of other body fluids are maintained. The secreted ions combine with the filtrate and form urine.
