The signs of a stitch are

(A) Vomiting & loss of memory

(B) Deformity in the abdomen

(C) Gasping for breath

(D) ** Pain in the side of the abdomen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A side stitch refers to pain felt on the side of the abdomen that occurs during physical activity. Though sometimes very painful, a side stitch is not harmful and does not require medical attention. Doctors sometimes call side stitches exercise-related transient abdominal pain (ETAP).

Concept note-2: -Side Stitch Causes If you have food in your stomach, your body will naturally pump more blood to that area as part of the digestion process. This means less blood in the diaphragm and a higher chance of diaphragm cramping. Another potential cause is the stretching of ligaments.

Concept note-3: -The exact cause of a side stitch is unknown. Some studies show that a movement of blood to the diaphragm or muscles during physical activity can lead to a side stitch. But other research shows that an irritation of the lining of the abdominal and pelvic cavity may be the cause.

Concept note-4: -Appendicitis typically starts with a pain in the middle of your tummy (abdomen) that may come and go. Within hours, the pain travels to your lower right-hand side, where the appendix is usually located, and becomes constant and severe. Pressing on this area, coughing or walking may make the pain worse.

Concept note-5: -If the side stitch persists for more than a week, you should also consult your doctor or physical therapist for further evaluation.
