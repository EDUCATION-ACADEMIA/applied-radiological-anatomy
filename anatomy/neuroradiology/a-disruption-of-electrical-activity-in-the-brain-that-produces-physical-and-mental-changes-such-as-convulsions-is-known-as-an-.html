A disruption of electrical activity in the brain that produces physical and mental changes, such as convulsions, is known as a(n) ____

(A) ** seizures

(B) IDD

(C) shingles

(D) meningocele

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Electrical discharges occur throughout the brain, causing a generalized tonic-clonic seizure.

Concept note-2: -In epilepsy the brain’s electrical rhythms have a tendency to become imbalanced, resulting in recurrent seizures. In patients with seizures, the normal electrical pattern is disrupted by sudden and synchronized bursts of electrical energy that may briefly affect their consciousness, movements or sensations.

Concept note-3: -Focal seizures result from electrical activity in one area of the brain. This type of seizure can occur with or without loss of consciousness: Focal seizures with impaired awareness.

Concept note-4: -The term “seizure” is often used interchangeably with “convulsion.” During convulsions a person has uncontrollable shaking that is rapid and rhythmic, with the muscles contracting and relaxing repeatedly. There are many different types of seizures. Some have mild symptoms without shaking.

Concept note-5: -(kun-VUL-zhun) A condition in which muscles contract and relax quickly and cause uncontrolled shaking of the body.
