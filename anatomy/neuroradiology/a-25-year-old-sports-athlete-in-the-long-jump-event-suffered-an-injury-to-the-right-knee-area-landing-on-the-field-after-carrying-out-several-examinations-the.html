After carrying out several examinations, the physiotherapist wanted to make a diagnosis of an anterior cruciate ligament injury, so the physiotherapist had to carry out specific tests. What is the name of the specific test on the athlete?

(A) Nery specific test

(B) Test Specific Burgard

(C) Ballotement specific test

(D) ** Slide drawer specific test

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A tear to the medial collateral ligament in the knee can cause pain, swelling, and a lack of stability in the knee. Treatment is usually with ice, a knee brace, and physical therapy. Surgery may be necessary in rare cases. The medial collateral ligament (MCL) connects the bones in the thigh and lower leg.

Concept note-2: -Injury to the infrapatellar branch of the saphenous nerve (IPBSN) is a high-frequency complication in anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) reconstruction.

Concept note-3: -The posterior cruciate ligament is the strongest and largest ligament in the knee. It runs diagonally down the back of your knee, connecting your femur to your tibia. 10 The main function of the PCL is to prevents your tibia from moving too far backward.
