TRUE or FALSE:Bones are living tissue.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bone is living tissue that makes up the bodyâ€™s skeleton. There are 3 types of bone tissue: Compact tissue. This is the harder, outer tissue of bones.

Concept note-2: -Tissue that gives strength and structure to bones. Bone is made up of compact tissue (the hard, outer layer) and cancellous tissue (the spongy, inner layer that contains red marrow). Bone tissue is maintained by bone-forming cells called osteoblasts and cells that break down bone called osteoclasts.

Concept note-3: -To respond to its dual roles of support and regulation of calcium and phosphorus, as well as to repair any damage to the skeleton, bone is constantly changing. Old bone breaks down and new bone is formed on a continuous basis. In fact, the tissue of the skeleton is replaced many times during life.
