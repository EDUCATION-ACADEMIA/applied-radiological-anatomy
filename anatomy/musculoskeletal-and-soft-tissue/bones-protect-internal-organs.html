Bones protect internal organs.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bones also protect the bodyâ€™s organs. The skull protects the brain and forms the shape of the face. The spinal cord, a pathway for messages between the brain and the body, is protected by the backbone, or spinal column.

Concept note-2: -Flat Bones Protect Internal Organs There are flat bones in the skull (occipital, parietal, frontal, nasal, lacrimal, and vomer), the thoracic cage (sternum and ribs), and the pelvis (ilium, ischium, and pubis). The function of flat bones is to protect internal organs such as the brain, heart, and pelvic organs.

Concept note-3: -The skeletal system protects the internal organs of the body. For example, ribcage protects the heart and lungs. Bones are moved by alternate contraction and relaxation of muscles that are attached to them.
