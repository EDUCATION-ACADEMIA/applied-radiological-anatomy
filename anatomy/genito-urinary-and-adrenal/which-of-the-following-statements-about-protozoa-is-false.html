Which of the following statements about protozoa is false?

(A) Protozoa may reproduce sexually.

(B) Protozoa may make cysts.

(C) Protozoa may have flagella or cilia.

(D) ** Protozoa may be prokaryotes.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The following statement regarding protozoa is false: a) Protozoa are multicellular eukaryotes. Protozoa are unicellular eukaryotes, which means they are made of only one cell. They are microorganisms, which means they can only be seen under a microscope.

Concept note-2: -False. Protozoans are not prokaryotic. How would you distinguish a large colonial organism from a multicellular organism?

Concept note-3: -All protozoans are eukaryotes and therefore possess a “true, ‘’ or membrane-bound, nucleus.

Concept note-4: -Protozoans are eukaryotic and unicellular. They live in water as well as in soil. Some of them are parasites found inside other organisms. Amoeba and Paramecium are few examples of protozoans.

Concept note-5: -The main difference between eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells is that eukaryotes contain membrane-bound organelles, whereas prokaryotes do not. As eukaryotic organisms, protists contain a nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles such as endoplasmic reticulum and Golgi apparatus.
